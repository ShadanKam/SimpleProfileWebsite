Misery

Author: Stephen King
Publisher: Addison Wisley
Year: 1978
Review:

Misery is very well written. King does a great job of demanding his reader's attention and keeping them hooked. Misery is quite fast paced for a novel. King never bogs his readers down with unnecessary detail. Every word earned a place.

The Lord of The Rings

Author: J. R. R Tolkiens
Publisher: Allen & Unwin
Year: 1954
Review:

I was introduced to this legend in the late nineties and remember buying the entire series with my internship money and reading The Hobbit before The Lord of The Rings. It's magical world pulled me instantly and I have been it's ardent fan. A monster of a book that I have read multiple times. I highly recommend this gem to anyone who loves a great saga.

C++ How to Program

Author: Paul and Harvey Deitel
Publisher: Prentice Hall
Year: 2004
Review:

The Deitel HOW TO PROGRAM series have been the leader for decades, in the field of teaching students how to quickly and thoroughly learn a programming language. Their books are thoroughly reviewed, and are continuously and significantly improved and updated with new editions every 2 years or so, based on current trends, insights and feedback.
